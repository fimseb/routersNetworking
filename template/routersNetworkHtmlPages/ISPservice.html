Virtual Network Operations License

Virtual Network Operator (VNO) or Mobile Virtual Network Operator (MVNO) is an internet service reseller and provides management services because they do not own telecommunication infrastructure. VNO’s are categorized as virtual networks because they do not possess actual network while offering these network services to their customers. VNO networks often lease bandwidth from various telecom providers at wholesale rates in order to provide solutions to their end customers.

  • Comparatively cost effective
  • Reduce network complexity
  • Reduce time to market
  • Reduced CAPAX
  • Increased flexibility
  • Quick formation/arrangement
  • No heavy investments on infra

VNO vs ISP

VNO License ISP License
VNO license is valid for 10 years ISP license is valid for 20 years
The license terms can be reviewed in every 4 years by DoT. Once issued, the license will be valid for 20 years no matter the changes in DoT policies.
Minor bank guarantee to be submitted More bank guarantees have to be submitted.
Lesser hardware involvement More hardware involvement
Get bandwidth purchase credit in AGR calculation Do not get bandwidth purchase amount credit in AGR for ISP
Can’t resell bandwidth in your service area to ISP or VNO company . Can resell bandwidth in your service area to ISP or VNO company.

How to acquire a VNO/ ISP license?

  • Check Eligibility: One has to register their company under the Companies Act, 1956. A Private. Limited Companies or Limited only .
  • VNO License Category : There are 3 categories of VNO/ UL ISP licenses. VNO license is cheapest than UL ISP.

    1. VNO Class A ISP (PAN India)
    2. VNO Class B ISP(Telecom Circle/Metro Area)
    3. VNO Class C ISP(Secondary Switching Area)

Budget and Cost Analysis: To augment internet services, the government kept the prices extremely low for category C in comparison to class A and B

NLD License

NLD License is the DOT issued authorization that gives the licensee the power of providing National Long-Distance Service. It means that through it, the company can provide telecommunication services over a long-distance network. That long distance network is one that connects many Short Distance Charging Areas (SDCAs).

A National Long Distance Provider, also known as NLDO is a telecom operator that provides NLD Service by providing the digital resources required to carry long distance telecom services on a national scale.
